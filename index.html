1.History

A small village in Senegal located about a dozen kilometers from the Senegal River and the border with the Mauritani. The village was founded around the 15th century by a Peul named Diakess. He left no descendants In the 2002 census, Kanel had 8,997 inhabitants, 501 concessions and 849 households. At the end of 2007, according to official estimates, the population would amount to 162,061 people. Kanel is located on the N2 national road, between Ourossogui and Semmé. The closest localities are Léwé N'Guénar, Léwé Damga, Faboli, Sintiane, Gana Balel, Mandioumba, Ganki Boubouyel, madina torobe. Foume Hara Diobe, Foume Hara Demboube, Soringho, Soringho Sebe and Seno Palel. The climate is Sahelian, hot and dry.
